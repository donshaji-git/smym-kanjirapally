Who We Are

About SMYM

Syro Malabar Youth Movement (SMYM) is the official youth movement of the Syro Malabar Church — one of the 23 Eastern Catholic Churches in full communion with Rome.

Founded to strengthen the spiritual, moral, and social life of Catholic youth, SMYM unites millions of young men and women across the globe under the banner of Christ's love. Through formation programs, community service, and vibrant gatherings, SMYM shapes the leaders of tomorrow's Church and society.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Mission

Bound Together for God's Kingdom

To bind together the youth of the Syro Malabar Church for the Kingdom of God and society, fostering unity, discipleship, and active participation in the life of the Church.

Vision

Integral Development in Faith

To foster the integral development of every young person — spiritually, morally, intellectually, and socially — rooted in the Gospel and the rich traditions of the Syro Malabar Church.

Our Journey

A Legacy of Faith

1971

Formation

SMYM is officially founded as the youth wing of the Syro Malabar Church, beginning its mission of faith formation and youth development.

1985

Diocesan Expansion

SMYM expands to all dioceses of the Syro Malabar Church, establishing a structured network of units, zones, and diocesan councils.

2000

Global Presence

With the Syro Malabar diaspora growing worldwide, SMYM chapters begin forming globally — in Europe, North America, and the Gulf.

2010

Social Impact

Launch of flagship social initiatives including anti-drug campus campaigns, de-addiction clubs, and leadership formation programs nationwide.

Today

Present Mission

SMYM stands as one of the world's largest Catholic youth movements with over 1.6 million members, continuing to transform lives through faith, fellowship, and formation.
